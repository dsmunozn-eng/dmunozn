Who I am

Scientist. Builder. Connector.

I am a bioengineer from Lima, Peru, with a deep commitment to democratizing science across Latin America. My work sits at the intersection of plant biotechnology, bioinformatics, and community-driven innovation — always asking how science can serve the people and ecosystems of the Andes.

As founder of Biotechnology Across The Andes, I lead the Plant Bioinformatics Study Group: a learning community making computational biology tools accessible to students and early-stage professionals across the region.

From building pH biosensors from native Peruvian potatoes to representing Latin America in the iGEM global ecosystem, I bring both wet-lab expertise and a systems-level vision for regional biotechnology development.
